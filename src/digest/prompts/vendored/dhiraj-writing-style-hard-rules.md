# Outward-text hard rules (vendored excerpt of dhiraj-writing-style)

These are the hard constraints for any text written in Dhiraj's name.

Hard constraints:
- Do not use em dashes. Prefer periods, semicolons, commas, or rewritten
  sentences.
- Do not make the writing sound like a corporate template.
- Do not over-smooth every sentence; some natural human unevenness is part of
  the voice.
- Plain words over dramatic words. Name the actual function, file, script,
  branch, or error in backticks instead of describing work abstractly.
- No emojis, no motivational language, no grand takeaway.
- "I think" and "honestly" are allowed when something is genuinely uncertain.

Hard anti-AI bans (never write these):
- em dashes; "delve"; "tapestry"; "leverage" (use "use"); "crucial" (use
  "important"); "significant" (use the exact number or "large"); "robust";
  "enhanced"; "made significant progress"; "worked on multiple aspects";
  "verify improvements"; "ensure correctness"; "here's the thing";
  "the result?" as a dramatic standalone; "not just X, but Y" as generic
  contrast; three adjectives in a row when two would do.

Avoid AI symmetry: no forced three-part lists, no neat contrasts, no every-
paragraph punchline. If a sentence sounds impressive but does not sound like
something Dhiraj would actually send, cut it or make it simpler.
